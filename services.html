Our Services

  • OSHA Auditing, compliance and training
    OSHA compliance and training encompasses workplace safety and health for all workers by meeting the standards set forth by Dept. of Labor. These standards were adopted and mandated in order to provide businesses a guideline from which to provide a minimum level of workplace safety for their employees. Training allows a chance for management and employees to be educated on those mandated standards that apply to their specific workplace environment.
  • OSHA program management
    OSHA program management is a function of the overall safety program and allows the safety professional to oversee the many facets of the program. Auditing, record keeping, training, environmental analysis are part of the program management.
    • Powered Industrial Trucks (Forklift)
    • Compliance Auditing
      Workplace auditing is essential for the safety professional to keep track of how the overall safety program is working within a particular business. There are many facets within the overall safety program that will need to be constantly reviewed in order for continued worker protection and business compliance.
    • Permit required confined space
    • Control of hazardous energy (lockout/tagout)
    • Hazardous materials management and response
    • Hazard Communication/Globally Harmonized Systems (Hazcom/GHS)
    • Trench/Excavation
    • Hot work permitting and management
  • Emergency response and planning
    Emergency response training and planning is an important part of the safety program for any company. A company must be able to react and respond in the event of an emergency to lessen the overall negative effect on employee health, as well as the overall business infrastructure. This includes dealing with the smallest of injury all the way up to a major incident.
  • Pre incident planning (Contingency Planning)
    Pre-Incident/Business Continuity planning allows the safety professional to identify all the risk a particular business could face. Then provide pro-active assistance eliminating or minimizing those identified risk(s). A few examples would include: utility outages, hostile event, fire, etc.
  • Post incident planning
    Post-Incident/Business Continuity planning allows the safety professional to create a plan to assist a business to react in the event of an emergency. There are many items in the event of an emergency that should be considered to ensure business continuity. Some examples of this would include: Staffing, logistics, clean up, human resources, etc.
  • Rescue stand by (Confined space entry)
    Project Safety Management/Rescue Stand By is a resource provided to assist a company in managing the safety and health of all personnel during projects outside of normal production. Some examples of this would include: confined space entry, hot work permitting, accident/injury investigation, first aid/medical care, etc. We have the knowledge, expertise, and can provide the man power to ensure compliance and the safety of all employees.
